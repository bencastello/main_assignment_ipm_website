The corridor of the old wizarding school was unusually silent. 
Dust drifted in the beams of torchlight, and the air tasted faintly of forgotten spells. 
Somewhere deep below, pipes groaned as if the building itself was waking from a long sleep.

A small shadow moved near a staircase. Not a monster this time. 
Just a student who stayed out far too late and regretted every life choice that led here.

Legends said the chamber could only be opened by someone who understood the language of serpents. 
The funny thing was: the walls had been whispering for years, but hardly anyone listened.

Behind a heavy stone archway, a faint glow pulsed like a heartbeat. 
It was unclear whether it was magic, danger, or simply the world's most aggressive nightlight.
